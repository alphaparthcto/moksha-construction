## Government Registration & Certifications

### SBA 8(a) Business Development Program — THE GOLDEN TICKET

**Why this matters**: Indian-American owners (Parth & Hari) qualify as "socially disadvantaged" under SBA rules. This unlocks:

- **Sole-source contracts up to $4.5 million** — no competitive bidding needed
- **Set-aside contracts** where only 8(a) firms can compete (way less competition)
- **Joint ventures** with large contractors on massive projects
- **Mentorship programs** paired with large established contractors
- **9-year program** with increasing contract thresholds

**Requirements**:
- At least 51% owned by socially/economically disadvantaged individuals
- Owner must have personal net worth under $850K (excluding primary residence and business)
- Business must have been in operation for at least 2 years
- Average annual gross receipts under $47.0M (for general construction NAICS 236220)

**How to apply**:
1. Go to certify.sba.gov
2. Gather: tax returns (3 years), financial statements, articles of incorporation, resumes
3. Application takes 2-3 months to process
4. APEX Accelerator will help with the application for FREE

### HUBZone Certification

- Check if Clarksville office is in a HUBZone (Historically Underutilized Business Zone)
- Fort Campbell's proximity may qualify the area
- Stacks with 8(a) for even more set-aside opportunities
- Check eligibility: map.sba.gov/hubzone

### MBE/DBE Certifications

| Certification | Issuing Agency | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **DBE** (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise) | TDOT | Required for highway/transit subcontracting goals |
| **MBE** (Minority Business Enterprise) | TN Governor's Office of Diversity | State contract preferences |
| **SBE** (Small Business Enterprise) | Various state/local | Local government contract preferences |
| **Go-DBE** | TDOT (unified portal) | One application covers DBE + SBE + MBE for TN |

**Apply for Go-DBE first** — it's a single application that covers multiple certifications through TDOT.

### State Contractor Licenses
- Display license numbers prominently on website (nobody in the market does this)
- Get licensed in expanding states: GA, SC, FL (if not already)
- Each state has different requirements — check reciprocity agreements

## Military Contracting — Fort Campbell

**Fort Campbell literally sits on the TN/KY border in Clarksville.** This is a massive opportunity nobody in the market is pursuing aggressively.

### Key Contracting Offices

| Office | What They Procure | How to Find Bids |
|---|---|---|
| **MICC Fort Campbell** (Mission & Installation Contracting Command) | Base facilities, barracks, maintenance, renovation | SAM.gov + FedConnect |
| **USACE Nashville District** | Major construction, flood control, environmental | SAM.gov (NAICS 236220) |
| **DLA** (Defense Logistics Agency) | Facility maintenance, utilities | SAM.gov |

### Active/Recent Fort Campbell Contracts

- **MATOC** (Multiple Award Task Order Contract): **$495 million** ceiling — general construction, renovation, repair at Fort Campbell
  - Multiple awardees share the work
  - Task orders range from $2K to $45M each
  - Current contract period through 2027+
- **JOC** (Job Order Contract): **$50 million** — smaller maintenance and repair jobs
  - Great entry point for new contractors
  - Unit price book system, fast turnaround
  - Projects typically $2K-$2M
- **IDIQ contracts** (Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity) — ongoing opportunities

### How to Win Military Work

1. **Register on SAM.gov** with NAICS codes:
   - 236220 — Commercial and Institutional Building Construction
   - 236210 — Industrial Building Construction
   - 238990 — All Other Specialty Trade Contractors
2. **Get on the Bidders List**: Email MICC Fort Campbell contracting office directly
3. **Attend Industry Days**: MICC holds pre-solicitation conferences — check SAM.gov for announcements
4. **Start small**: Bid on JOC task orders ($2K-$2M) to build past performance
5. **Past performance is everything** in federal contracting — build it up with small wins

### SBA 8(a) + Fort Campbell = Cheat Code

With 8(a) certification:
- Fort Campbell can award **sole-source contracts up to $4.5M** directly to Moksha
- No competitive bidding — just negotiate the price
- The contracting officer WANTS to use 8(a) firms (it helps their small business goals)
- Combined with HUBZone (if eligible), this is almost unfair
